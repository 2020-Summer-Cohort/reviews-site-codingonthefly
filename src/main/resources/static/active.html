Bruce Peninsula Review

The Bruce Peninsula is an astounding area to visit if you enjoy hiking and nature. The winding pine forest trails and towering cliffs into crystal clear water demand you explore them. After exerting yourself in your travels, The Fish And Chip Place serves amazing white-fish tacos(And I don’t even like fish!)

Gettysburg Review

Rated number one on things to do in Gettysburg, Pennsylvania, this historic military landmark was once a major turning point in the Civil War. Thought to have been one of the bloodiest battles during the Civil War, this was also a pivotal moment in history that gave inspiration for Abraham Lincoln’s “Gettysburg Address”. Many locals say that this landmark is also extremely haunted. For anyone who is an avid golfer, this links-style course presents a demanding layout with unforgiving fairways and greens, as well as various steep rock cliffs, lakes, and waterfalls spanning across its 18 holes. At a length of 7069 yards, this par-72 course is one of the larger public courses in the area, making it a challenge for some golfers off the tee. After the turn, or at the end of your round, The Links At Gettysburg also has a spacious indoor/outdoor bar with great food and beverages.

Miami Review

A historical Italian estate first built in 1916 as a private winter retreat for American Businessman James Deering, this beautiful villa pays tribute to the Italian Renaissance era. On this site tourists can visit a Museum that still has its earlier furnishings and artwork intact, as well as its beautiful formal gardens. Many tourists say this is one of the best bay views in Miami! Open to the public, and free of charge! Doesn’t get any better than that. Wynwood Walls was first established and opened in 2009 by well-known art visionary Tony Goldman. This outdoor museum features more than 40 street art murals from some of the leading street artists around the world. If you’re an up-and-coming artist looking for inspiration, this place is a must see!

Stratford Review

Stratford is a gorgeous little town just north of the border. If you are able to visit during the annual Shakespeare festival you'll be treated to a plethora of wonderful stage performances, and if that is not quite your style I must recommend the quiet joy of a candied apple while strolling the river Avon.
